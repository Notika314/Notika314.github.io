Thinking style and learning.

December 5, 2014

As I took the test to determine my thinking style,it showed that I'm in-between Concrete Random and Abstract Sequential styles( kind of weird combination to me.)

So, on one side, I like analyzing situations before finding solutions to the problems( true),on the other hand, there are situations( usually when I don't have enough materials to learn from or when I'm stuck with a problem,) I do like to experiment and take risks.

I supppose it is a good thing that i don't limit myself to one way of solving problem,but I might sometimes try to understand material better before diving into my "experimental mode" and trying everything out.

Another thing i want to work on,is to be able to go back to already completed problem in order to redo or improve it( I really hate to do that).

Also, I know I tend to panick if something seems to difficult. That's what I am working on now. I found that if I read the problem , than leave it for a while , letting my brain to process it while I do something else ( work out, sleep, or go out), then come back to it later, my understanding of a topic usually goes further, and I can start work on it. As well I break it into smaller parts, so i don't feel intimidated by how much there is to accomplish.

But other than that, I am pretty happy with how my brain processes new information. As long as I understand something, I am able to connect it to other subjects, like puzzle piece, to get "big picture", and that helps me to remember it better.

And of course, we should not limit ourselves to what tests say, or to how we are able to process material now. To improve my way of learning , I've been trying new tricks lately, like "Pomodoro", creating to-do lists; and I want try some other.
