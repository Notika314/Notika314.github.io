Post 4.Pairing sessions and receiving feedback

January 2, 2015

PAIRING SESSIONS

To be honest, I was kind of sceptical about pairing sessions entering Phase 0. Most of my school or college time I found myself enjoying solving problems on my own, so that I can take full credit for my accomplishments, and also I felt that if I work with someone who does not understand the subject enough,I have to slow down; and when working with someone who is good with the topic, it often get more to competing than cooperaring.

But I found it different here. The difference probably is because in programming there are so many different ways to complete same problem, so I find myself being able both to benefit to pairing session by learning something new, and contribute to it. Most of the times I feel eally impressed with how much I learn from person I work with. And I find that I finish problems way faster while pairing, because there is a timeboxing there, and I still work on being able to timebox myself through studying.

So that's on the fun part.

Difficult part for me is to be able to admit when I am struggling and ask questions, so that i really learn instead of just follow my pair's insructions. That is what I try to work on.
